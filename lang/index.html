Language

Our language is Shangrilan. Itâ€™s an alphabet of symbols made up of different combinations of lines and dots. Each character is based on the same shape: four-line segments that meet in the middle, with dots in the corners between lines. Numbers use horizontal and vertical lines, and letters use a diagonal system.

Excerpt from the journal of a Shangrilan Fisherman

June 21

The summer season is finally here, but the roads to the high altitudes have only opened in the past week. Most mountaintops are still covered in many feet of snow, but the snow removal systems are now able to keep the roads open. Most of the year, our fishing is mainly limited to the major rivers that flow through our cities. But as the snow melts, the fishermen are able to move upstream to catch the fish in our high alpine lakes, which are considered a summertime delicacy. This summer, my partners and I plan to trek into the mountains to fish the glacial streams and Azure lake. We hope to return with a heavy load of cutthroat trout before our trails are snow-covered once again.
